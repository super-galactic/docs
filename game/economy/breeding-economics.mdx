Breeding is one of Super Galactic’s most economically impactful systems. It converts UAP into controlled character expansion, which increases gameplay capacity, deepens progression demand, and supports a sustainable player-driven marketplace

Breeding is designed around a simple economic loop: players spend UAP to create new playable characters, then reinvest UAP to develop, trade, or breed further, with all spending feeding burn and Game Treasury flows.

### Why Breeding Matters Economically

Breeding strengthens the economy in three ways:

1. **Token demand:** breeding requires UAP at the point of execution
2. **Supply control:** new characters can only be created through controlled generational rules and capped attempts
3. **Reinvestment loops:** new characters restart progression, creating additional long-term UAP demand through upgrades and development

This makes breeding a high-commitment sink that scales with engaged communities rather than passive participation.

### The Breeding Loop

Breeding creates a repeatable gameplay economy loop without unlimited expansion:

1. A player spends UAP to breed a new character
2. Spending triggers the burn and Game Treasury split defined in [Burn Mechanisms](https://supergalactic.mintlify.app/game/economy/burn-mechanisms)
3. The new character increases roster capacity and long-term progression potential
4. The player may choose to upgrade the new character, creating additional UAP demand
5. The player may also breed again using remaining attempts, reinforcing the cycle

This loop supports growth while keeping both token and asset expansion controlled.

## Player Strategies and Market Dynamics

Breeding supports multiple valid player paths, each contributing to UAP demand and ecosystem activity.

### 1. Roster Expansion Strategy

Owners who breed to expand their roster increase their total gameplay capacity.

Because reward eligibility is capped per Genesis within a rolling 24-hour window, additional characters increase the number of reward-eligible missions a player can complete over time.

### 2. Breeder-to-Market Strategy

Some owners may breed early and sell offspring on the marketplace

This enables:

- New player onboarding without requiring Genesis ownership
- Player-driven distribution of new characters
- Secondary market activity alongside continued UAP spending

This strategy creates demand on both sides: UAP demand through breeding and progression, and marketplace demand through acquisition.

### 3. Collector and Hybrid Strategy

Breeding also functions as a collectible system through genetic inheritance and hybrid outcomes.

Hybrid combinations introduce:

- Unique visual identities
- Mixed Origin traits
- Scarce “pure” outcomes alongside rarer collectible mixes

Collectors may breed for scarcity and identity rather than maximum gameplay efficiency. Many collectors also upgrade offspring to increase their strength and long-term value, adding further UAP demand.

### Cost Scaling and Balance Controls

Breeding costs are predefined but configurable to support long-term balance.

Parameters can be adjusted post-launch based on:

- Live gameplay analytics
- On-chain and in-game activity patterns
- Supply growth and player onboarding trends

The goal is to keep breeding:

- Economically meaningful
- Accessible enough to support growth
- Controlled enough to prevent excessive expansion

<Note>
  Any material changes to breeding parameters will be communicated transparently through official development updates
</Note>

### Burn and Game Treasury Impact

All UAP spent on breeding triggers the burn and Game Treasury split defined in Burn Mechanisms. As breeding activity increases, total burn volume increases proportionally, linking ecosystem growth directly to usage-based supply reduction.

Detailed execution is documented in [Burn Mechanisms](https://supergalactic.mintlify.app/game/economy/burn-mechanisms).

### System Constraints That Preserve Sustainability

Breeding remains sustainable because it is bounded by multiple system-level constraints:

- Fixed Genesis supply
- Limited breeding attempts per Genesis (up to 7)
- Strict generational rules (Genesis → Alpha/Beta only)
- Increasing cost per attempt
- Burn enforcement on every spend

These constraints ensure the economy can grow through player activity without allowing runaway asset inflation.
